Sky

Epipelagic Layer

This is the zone in which photosynthesis can occur, because light is available.

Mesopelagic Layer

This is also known as the twilight zone, because light becomes limited. There is less oxygen available to organisms in this zone.

Bathypelagic Layer

This is a dark zone where water pressure is high and the water is cold (around 35-39 degrees).

Abyssopelagic Layer

This is the zone past the continental slope - the deep water just over the ocean bottom. This is also known as the abyssal zone.

Hadopelagic Layer

In some places, there are trenches that are deeper than the surrounding ocean floor. These areas are the hadopelagic zone. At a depth of over 36,000 feet, the Mariana Trench is the deepest known point in the ocean.
